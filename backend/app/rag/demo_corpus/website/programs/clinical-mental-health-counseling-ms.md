# Clinical Mental Health Counseling, MS

The Clinical Mental Health Counseling, MS program prepares students for advanced study in counseling theory, helping relationships, assessment, ethics, and supervised field experience.

## Program overview

The curriculum is structured in a deliberate sequence. Students complete foundational counseling courses before field-experience courses, and they should plan for supervised practice requirements that may affect scheduling.

## What you will study

- Counseling theories and helping skills
- Human development and assessment
- Group counseling and crisis response
- Ethics, diversity, and professional identity
- Practicum and internship preparation

## Format and pacing

The program is primarily online with required synchronous and field-experience components. Students should discuss state expectations and field-placement planning before choosing a start date.

## Licensure note

This program is designed to support educational preparation for counseling licensure, but licensure requirements vary by state. Students must review state requirements and speak with Admissions about their intended state of practice.
